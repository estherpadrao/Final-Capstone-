Hidden Trends — Batch Scenario Analysis

A single scenario tells you what happens in one place. Hidden Trends runs many random scenarios at once — across different parts of the city and at different scales — and looks for patterns in the results. The city's hexes are split into three score bands (Low · Mid · High). Random starting hexes are drawn from each band, and the chosen scenario is applied repeatedly. The three charts reveal: whether interventions in underserved areas move the city more than those in already well-connected areas; whether scale (single hex vs. small cluster) amplifies or dampens the effect; and whether each run tends to produce a net city gain or a net loss. The random seed pins which specific hexes are selected — keeping the seed fixed lets you compare different scenario types on the exact same starting locations; changing the seed draws a new random sample to verify that patterns are stable.
