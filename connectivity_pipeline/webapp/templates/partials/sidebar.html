What do these buttons do?

▶ PCI runs the full People Connectivity Index pipeline for the selected city: downloads OSM amenities, builds the walk/transit/drive network, computes Hansen accessibility, and scores every hex cell.

▶ BCI runs the Business Connectivity Index: fetches census income & labour data, maps commercial suppliers from OSM, then computes market, labour, and supplier accessibility for each hex.

▶ Run Both executes PCI then BCI sequentially in one click — useful when you want a full picture of a city.

Restore Saved Results reloads a previously computed result from disk without re-running the pipeline — much faster if you just refreshed the page or want to return to the last saved state.

What are these parameters?

Hansen β is the exponential decay rate that controls how quickly accessibility falls off with travel time. A higher β means people are less willing to walk far — only nearby amenities count much. Lower β gives more weight to distant amenities. Default 0.08 reflects typical urban behaviour.

Active Street λ adds a bonus for streets with high observed pedestrian activity (e.g. commercial frontage). Increase it to reward vibrant street life; set to 0 to use pure accessibility.

Amenity Weights control the relative importance of each category in the composite mass score. Defaults follow Zheng et al. (2021). Adjust if your city has a different planning priority — e.g. raise Parks weight in a city emphasising green space.

OSM Tags determine which OpenStreetMap categories are fetched as amenities. Un-checking a tag completely excludes that category from the analysis.

What are these parameters?

β Market sets how quickly customer demand decays with travel time. Higher values mean businesses mainly attract local customers; lower values allow a wider catchment area. Default 0.12 reflects typical retail shopping behaviour.

β Labour controls worker commute tolerance. A higher value means workers strongly prefer jobs close to home. Default 0.05 is more lenient, reflecting that people commute further for work than for shopping.

β Supplier governs business-to-business service decay — how far firms reach for inputs and professional services. Default 0.10.

Interface λ gives a bonus to hexes at urban-edge locations (near airports, major freight interchanges). Increase it if edge-of-city logistics hubs should score higher.

Combination Method sets how the three components (market, labour, supplier) are combined into a single BCI score. Weight-free treats them equally; Weighted lets you set custom proportions if, for example, labour access matters most in your study area.

OSM Supplier Tags determine which commercial/industrial categories are counted as business suppliers when computing supplier accessibility.
